**Base checkpoint**: hybrid_ri4_cont_step590.pt (from previous best 0.40 training)
**Horizon**: 50 steps continuation (590 → 640)
**Common setup**: EqR H/L (H=3/L=8), Anderson in L, internal_fast_recurrent, brain_triple_memory, strong_protection

## Root Cause Analysis (Key Finding)

The dominant factor was the **interaction between high internalization and aggressive depth_consistency_weight**.

In the code (train_hybrid_ri4_real_continuation_minimal.py), when `internal_fast_recurrent=True`:

- Original safe behavior (0.40 run): depth_consistency_weight auto-set to **0.06**
- My earlier edit (active for 0.42 and 0.45): when internalization ≥ 0.40, auto-set to **0.15** (or 0.10)

This created **double high pressure** in short horizon:
- Very high attractor internalization (0.42 or 0.45)
- Very high depth consistency pressure (0.10~0.15)

In a short 50-step continuation, this over-constrained the model, preventing proper basin shaping and causing late-run collapse instead of the desired "deeper-is-better" inductive bias.

The 0.40 run benefited from milder consistency pressure (only 0.06), allowing the internalization to do its job without over-constraining the system.

## Lessons

1. Internalization weight is extremely sensitive in short-horizon continuations.
2. Combining high internalization with high depth_consistency is dangerous without proper ramping or longer training.
3. The "more pressure = better RI-1" intuition failed here when pushed beyond the 0.40 sweet spot under current setup.
4. The auto depth_consistency boost I introduced was overly aggressive and contributed to the 0.42/0.45 failures.

## Recommended Next Directions

- Fix the auto depth_consistency logic (already done - made more conservative).
- Test 0.41 with controlled depth_consistency (0.07~0.08).
- Consider pressure ramping for internalization instead of step jumps.
- Re-evaluate whether depth_consistency should be the primary lever or kept mild.
